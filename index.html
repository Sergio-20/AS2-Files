Fun Facts about Edmond, OK:

Edmond is the home town of Olympic gymnast Shannon Miller, America's most decorated Olympic gymnast. In the 1992 Summer Olympics she won 2 silver and 3 bronze medal, winning 2 gold medals in 1996. You might find the Interstate Highway 35 here which has been named the Shannon Miller Parkway.

On August 6, 2008 Edmond hosted a memorial service for famed Oklahoman baseball player Bobby Murcer. Reggie Jackson, Derek Jeter, and Andy Pettitte were just a few of the 2,000 people who attended.

Edmond was featured in Newsmax magazine's list of “Top 25 most uniquely American Cities and Towns” in 2009 for Edmond's 10-day long LibertyFest event which includes the state's largest parade, fireworks, a beauty pageant, a kite festival, and a rodeo.

The Santa Fe rail line established Edmond as a water and coaling station for steam engines in 1887. Now it is home to around 90,000 US citizens. There is plenty to do in Edmond, from swimming to ice skating, culture, entertainment and leisure. Edmond, OK has a high employment rate and affordable living potential. Edmond is a nice, clean, all American town that is perfect for families and young people.
