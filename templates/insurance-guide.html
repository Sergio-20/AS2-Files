Insurance Guide, A to Z

Do you need self storage insurance?

You should check with your homeowner's insurance policy, but most of the time once an item leaves your house it is no longer covered. Storage facilities are typically safe however accidents and theft can still happen in spite of our best efforts. If you are concerned about potentially losing your valuables then you should consider purchasing insurance. You can check with your personal insurance provider to see if there are any options that they offer. If not then most facilities will offer policies.

All American Self Storage facilities are Uhaul affiliates so our customers have the option to be insured under Safestor®. Safestor provides the broadest coverage and lowest premiums so you can have peace of mind knowing that your goods will be replaced should there be an unfortunate occurrence while they are in storage.

Who is responsible?

American Self Storage strives to provide safe, clean, and secure storage facilities for its customers, however we are not responsible for loss or damages. Please don't assume that you are covered and be sure to check with your insurance carrier in regards to coverage.

What does Safestor® cover?

Safestor covers loss or damage caused by:

  • Fire
  • Lightning
  • Tornado
  • Burglary – Must be visible signs of forced entry and a police report must be filed.
  • Earthquake
  • Explosion
  • Leaking water
  • Smoke
  • Hurricane
  • Wind
  • Hail
  • Building Collapse
  • Vandalism
  • Vermin

items not covered by Safestor:

  • Jewelry
  • Currency
  • Works of art
  • Securities
  • watches
  • Furs
  • Mortorized vehicle (Licensed or not, such as motor scooters, ATVs, mortorcycles, and.or lawnmowers)
  • Financial records
  • Deeds & documents
  • Antiques
  • Stamps
  • Precious Stones
  • Garments trimmed w/ fur
  • No coverage for loss caused by or resulting from flood (or surface water) or climatic conditions like rust or mildew.
